About Us

Welcome to Wawa! A charming little town located on Wawa Lake, just off the coast of Lake Superior. You may know us from our magnificant Wawa Goose that has overlooked Highway 17 since 1963. First established as a fur trade fort in the 17th century, the town is now known for it's picturesque scenic surroundings and is, for many, a favourite Canadian road side stop in the summer months. Whether you're looking for trails to hike, beaches to relax on, or waterfalls to chase, Wawa has something for everyone!
If you're thinking the town quiets down in the winter, you'd be half right. For sure, the tourist industry dies down in the winter, with the freezing temperatures, icy roads and Lake Superior's firdged winds. However, the locals have no problem embracing the cold. You'll find many of the towns residents snowshoeing on snow packed trails, riding snowmobiles, ice fishing on the frozen Wawa Like, or even dog sledding around town.
The bottomline? Hot or cold, rain or shine, Wawa is the perfect get-away for anyone looking to escape the hustle and bustle of cities and enjoy the peace and serenidty of Northern Ontario.

Our History

Michipicoten First Nation History

Wawa and it's surrounding area is rich in indigenous history. It has been proven that the Anishinaabeg and Ojibway people have occupied the region for over 7,000 years. Ancient canoe routes show that the mouth of the Micipicoten and Magpie Rivers were a hub of transporation with access to what is today northern ontario. These routes connected to other Great Lakes and the inland sea of Hudson's Bay. The michipicoten tribe was made up of several different family groups who lived the traditional way of life. They were dispersed over a large area of several hundred square miles, gathering in the summer months to make alliances of trade, amrriages and other shared ceremonies. Township History

With the first explorers also came economic opportunities in the fur trade. The Michipicoten First Nation's introduced the early explorers to the nearby forests in the early 1600's. Not only were our forests fur-rich, but Michipicoten was the ideal location for a post trade with the Ojibway as a rest and re-supply stop for travelers. In 1827, Michipicoten was name headquarters for the Lake Superior Distract of Hudson's Bay Company. The post was closed after 179 operative years in 1904.
Forestry came in 1900 when pulpwood and logs for railways ties were cut right along the Algoma Central Railway. Many people from Eastern Canada found winter work in the nearby logging camps. Tree's were fell by hand and hauled by horses on icy winter roads and harvested wood was piles onto frozen lakes. Forestry continues to play a role in Wawa's economy to this very day.
When Ojibway couple Louise Towab and William Teddy uncovered a rich vein of gold along Wawa Lake in 1897, Wawa boomed. The next hundred years, Wawa was the centre of mining activity in Northern Ontario. The gold discovery lead to the discovery of Iron ore in 1898. During the World War Two, a singer plant was built in Wawa to treat to iron from Helene Mine. During this time, wages were high and job were in demand and Wawa became home for many European immigrants after the end of the war. While Iron mining is no longer the main source of Wawa's survival, gold and diamonds continue to drive Wawa's economy forward.

Summer Activities

  • Visit Sandy Beach

    • If long, rockless beaches are the things for you, then you can't miss a drive out to Sandy Beach. Only about 20 minutes outside of Wawa, Sandy Beach is Wawa's very own tropical getaway. Although some say the water is too cold to swim in until August, you'll find locals enjoying this portion of Lake Superior as early as May (weather depending)
    Whether you're looking for a place to bring to family or for some personal nature driven solitude, Sandy Beach is a must for Wawa visitors!

  • Scenic Highfalls

    • Packed full with history, Scenic High Falls is a great place to have a picture, take some photos and learn about our community! Located just off the highway coming from Sault Ste Marie, the falls are just a five minute dirt road drive away!

  • Agawa Rock Pictograps

    • Big history buff with a love for hiking? The Agawa Rock Pictographs is the place for you. See ancient Indigenous pictographs that have endured centuries of harsh lake superior elements. That being said, this is a sacred place, so be respectful and don't touch the rock paintings.

  • Naturally Superior Adventures

    • Paddle the breathtaking lake superior with Naturally Superior Adventures lessons and trips. Whether you're a Stand Up Paddling pro or a total Kayak newbie, NSA is a great way to spend a day in Wawa!

  • Michipicoten Golf Course

    • FORE! Convieniently located down the hill from town, this nature nestled 9 hole course is perfect for those looking to work on their backswing. If you're not much of a golfer, but are friends with golfers, don't worry, the beverage cart comes around regularly to keep you occupied,

Winter Activities

  • Snowmobiling

    • With over 400km of fine groomer trails, there is no better way to experience to beauty that Winter in Wawa has to offer. Often referred to as a Sledders Paradise, Wawa's snowmobile season lasts well in March. So suit up, pack up the sled and let's go for a rip eh bud?

  • Ice Fishing

    • Really, fishing is a year round activity here in Wawa. With over 12,000 inland lakes there are plenty are fishing spots to choose from, including Lake Superior herself. So snow, rain or shine, pack your gear, make a stop at Young's General Store for the bait and lets get fishing!

  • Skiing & Snowshoeing

    • Of course, with endless amounts of hiking trails, rest assured there are plenty of places to enjoy the wintery forest wonderland. Trails behind our airport are great for cross country skiier and snowshoers alike.
